# Project Bible

## 1. Project thesis
Ash Archive treats Vvardenfell as already haunted by prophecy, reincarnation, and suppressed history. The island remembers the player before the player remembers themselves.

## 2. Dual-edition philosophy
Two editions share aesthetic pillars and narrative logic while using engine-native techniques.

## 3. Why OpenMW and MWSE are sibling projects, not identical load orders
OpenMW prioritizes runtime stability and long-play consistency. MWSE enables reactive scripted horror systems. Forcing parity would weaken both.

## 4. Pilgrim Edition design rules
- Prioritize stable systems and clean traversal.
- Emphasize weather, distance, documents, tomb architecture, and retrospective dread.
- Prefer environmental pressure over scripted jump events.

## 5. Sleeper Edition design rules
- Use MWSE scripting for dream contamination and reactive dread.
- Build identity fracture through event timing, ritual repetition, and sleep-state pressure.
- Keep systems readable and configurable; avoid chaotic script stacking.

## 6. Non-goals
- No direct crossover IP content.
- No claim of final compatibility before verification.
- No convenience-first overhaul that erases pilgrimage friction.

## 7. Design pillars
- **The Island Remembers**
- **The Dream is Geological**
- **Reincarnation is Body Horror**
- **Dagoth Ur is Intimate, Not Loud**
- **The Tribunal Are the Beautiful Crime Scene**
- **Evidence Before Explanation**

## 8. Psychological horror translation notes
### Silent Hill 2 logic translated into Morrowind terms
Guilt is spatial: foyadas, tomb complexes, and canton interiors should feel like moral topography.

### David Lynch logic translated into Morrowind terms
Use dream causality, doubles, odd repetition, and mundane uncanny timing through native lore and quest framing.

### Lake Mungo logic translated into Morrowind terms
Archive-first design: logs, notes, shrines, and rumors should foreshadow dread before explicit revelation.

## 10. Core atmosphere rules
- no direct horror-franchise crossover content
- no generic jumpscare logic
- no Skyrimification
- no anime face replacers
- no convenience fast travel that destroys pilgrimage/travel design
- no survival mechanics that become sandwich accounting
- horror should emerge from Morrowind's own systems and lore
